Understanding TV Energy Ratings in Australia

Australia's energy rating system for televisions provides consumers with important information about the energy efficiency of different models. The data collected includes various metrics that help determine a television's star rating.

Screen Technology

The type of screen technology (LCD, Plasma, OLED, etc.) significantly impacts energy consumption. Newer technologies like LED-backlit LCDs are generally more efficient than older plasma screens.

Screen Size & Area

Measured diagonally in centimeters, screen size directly affects energy use. Larger screens consume more power, which is why energy ratings consider both screen size and area (height × width).

Standby Power Modes

Modern TVs have two standby modes:

  • Passive Standby: Low-power state for receiving remote signals
  • Active Standby: Higher-power state for network connectivity and updates

Average On-Mode Power

This measurement indicates the energy consumed when the television is actively displaying content. It's the primary factor in determining energy efficiency during use.

Comparative Energy Consumption (CEC)

Expressed as kilowatt-hours per year, the CEC provides an estimate of annual energy use based on typical viewing patterns, allowing for easy comparison between models.

Star Rating System

The star rating (ranging from 1 to 10 stars) indicates relative efficiency within a product category. More stars mean better energy efficiency and lower operating costs.

How to Choose an Energy-Efficient Television

1. Check the Star Rating

Look for models with higher star ratings. Each additional star represents significant energy savings over the lifetime of the television.

2. Consider Screen Size

Choose an appropriately sized screen for your viewing distance. Larger screens consume more energy, so avoid oversized televisions.

3. Evaluate Standby Power

Look for models with low standby power consumption, especially if you frequently use smart features that require maintaining network connectivity.

4. Compare CEC Values

Use the Comparative Energy Consumption figure to estimate yearly operating costs based on your electricity rate.

5. Consider Screen Technology

LED-backlit LCD TVs generally offer the best balance of picture quality and energy efficiency for most consumers.

Australian Television Energy Regulations

Televisions sold in Australia must meet Minimum Energy Performance Standards (MEPS) set by the Australian Government. These regulations:

  • Ensure all televisions meet minimum efficiency requirements
  • Provide consistent energy rating labels to help consumers compare models
  • Encourage manufacturers to improve the energy efficiency of their products
  • Contribute to Australia's overall energy reduction goals

Products are registered in the GEMS (Greenhouse and Energy Minimum Standards) database, which tracks compliance with these regulations.
